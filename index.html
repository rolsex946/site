About Dr. Hakam Singh

A dedicated orthopaedic surgeon committed to restoring mobility and improving lives through surgical excellence.

Dr. Hakam Singh

MBBS, MS (Orth), Fellowship (Arthroscopy & Sports Medicine)

Profile & Credentials

Dr. Hakam Singh is a Consultant Orthopaedic Surgeon & Sports Injury Specialist with extensive training from some of India's most respected medical institutions. After completing his MBBS from GMC Amritsar in 2012, he pursued MS in Orthopedics from DMC Ludhiana (2014-2017), building a strong foundation in all aspects of orthopedic surgery.

Driven by a passion for sports medicine and minimally invasive surgery, he completed his superspecialisation in Arthroscopy and Sports Medicine from Ortho One Hospital, Coimbatore in 2021 — one of India's premier sports medicine centers. This advanced training equipped him with cutting-edge skills in arthroscopic procedures for the knee, shoulder, ankle, and other joints.

He worked at SPS Hospital, Ludhiana for one year as an Orthopedics and Sports Medicine Consultant, gaining valuable experience in managing complex cases across the full spectrum of orthopedics. After gaining enough experience in all fields, he returned to his hometown to serve the people here — bringing world-class orthopedic care closer to home.

His approach to patient care combines evidence-based medicine with individualized treatment planning. He believes every patient deserves a clear explanation of their condition, a thorough discussion of all treatment options, and a surgeon who genuinely cares about their outcome and return to an active lifestyle.

Arthroscopy & Sports Medicine

Dr. Hakam Singh's primary area of superspecialisation. Arthroscopic (keyhole) surgery allows diagnosis and treatment of joint problems through tiny incisions, resulting in less pain, minimal scarring, and faster recovery. His fellowship at Ortho One Hospital, Coimbatore provided intensive training in the most advanced arthroscopic techniques.

His sports medicine practice covers the full spectrum of athletic injuries — from weekend warriors to competitive athletes — with a focus on returning patients to their sport at pre-injury performance levels through evidence-based rehabilitation protocols.

  • ACL Reconstruction (Anatomic Technique)
  • Meniscus Repair & Partial Meniscectomy
  • Rotator Cuff Repair
  • Cartilage Restoration (Microfracture, MACI)
  • Shoulder, Ankle & Elbow Arthroscopy
  • Sports Injury Prevention Programs

Joint Replacement Surgery

Total and partial knee and hip replacement surgery using the latest generation of implants and minimally invasive surgical approaches. Dr. Hakam Singh combines his training in joint replacement from DMC Ludhiana with modern techniques for optimal outcomes.

Conditions

Conditions Treated

Expert diagnosis and treatment for a wide range of musculoskeletal conditions.

ACL Tear

Anterior Cruciate Ligament injury — one of Dr. Hakam Singh's most commonly treated conditions. Causes knee instability during pivoting activities. Treated with anatomic arthroscopic ACL reconstruction using hamstring autograft.

View Treatments →

Meniscus Tear

Tear of the knee's shock-absorbing cartilage. Can occur from sports or degenerative changes. Treatment depends on tear pattern — repair for vascular zones, partial meniscectomy for degenerative tears.

View Treatments →

Rotator Cuff Tear

Tear of shoulder tendons causing pain, weakness, and limited range of motion. Common in overhead athletes and older adults. Arthroscopic repair provides excellent outcomes with proper rehabilitation.

View Treatments →

Osteoarthritis

Degenerative joint disease causing cartilage loss, pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. Treatment ranges from lifestyle modification and physiotherapy to joint replacement surgery for advanced cases.

View Treatments →

Patellofemoral Pain

"Runner's knee" — the most common cause of knee pain in young adults. Pain in the front of the knee aggravated by stairs, squatting, and sitting. Usually managed with targeted physiotherapy.

Consult Doctor →

Fractures & Dislocations

All types of fractures including simple, comminuted, intra-articular, and open fractures. Treated with casting, internal fixation (plates, nails, screws), or external fixation.

View Treatments →

Disc Herniation

Spinal disc bulge causing nerve compression, back pain, and sciatica. Most cases improve with conservative treatment. Surgical microdiscectomy for persistent neurological deficits.

View Treatments →

Cartilage Injuries

Damage to articular cartilage from trauma or degeneration. Treatment options include arthroscopic chondroplasty, microfracture, and cartilage restoration procedures for young patients.

View Treatments →

Tendinopathies

Tendon disorders including Achilles tendinopathy, patellar tendinopathy ("jumper's knee"), and rotator cuff tendinopathy. Managed with structured rehabilitation, injection therapy, and occasionally surgery.

Consult Doctor →
Procedures

Surgical Procedures

Detailed information about the surgical interventions offered by Dr. Hakam Singh.

ACL Reconstruction

Arthroscopic reconstruction of the torn ACL using hamstring autograft. Dr. Hakam Singh follows an anatomic reconstruction technique — placing the graft at the exact footprint of the native ACL for optimal stability and knee kinematics. Performed through small incisions with camera guidance. Day-care or 1-day hospital stay.

Duration

1 — 1.5 hours

Hospital Stay

Same day / 1 day

Return to Sport

6 — 9 months

Meniscus Repair / Meniscectomy

Arthroscopic meniscus repair for tears in the vascular zone (preserving the meniscus for long-term joint health) or partial meniscectomy for tears in non-repairable zones. Dr. Hakam Singh prioritizes meniscus preservation whenever possible, as loss of meniscal tissue accelerates arthritis.

Duration

45 min — 1.5 hours

Hospital Stay

Same day

Recovery

2 weeks — 4 months

Rotator Cuff Repair

Arthroscopic repair of torn rotator cuff tendons using suture anchors. The torn tendon is reattached to its footprint on the humerus with secure fixation. Sling immobilization for 4-6 weeks followed by structured rehabilitation. Full recovery takes 6-12 months.

Duration

1 — 2 hours

Hospital Stay

Same day / 1 day

Full Recovery

6 — 12 months

Total Knee Replacement (TKR)

Damaged knee joint surfaces are replaced with metal and plastic implants. Modern surgical approaches minimize tissue damage, reduce blood loss, and accelerate rehabilitation. Most patients begin walking within 24 hours.

Duration

1.5 — 2 hours

Hospital Stay

3 — 5 days

Recovery

6 — 12 weeks

Total Hip Replacement (THR)

Femoral head and acetabulum are replaced with prosthetic components. Both cemented and uncemented techniques available based on patient age, bone quality, and activity level.

Duration

1.5 — 2.5 hours

Hospital Stay

4 — 7 days

Recovery

8 — 12 weeks

Fracture Fixation (ORIF)

Open Reduction and Internal Fixation — surgically realigning fractured bones and securing them with plates, screws, or intramedullary nails using biologically respectful techniques that promote faster bone healing.

Duration

Varies by fracture

Hospital Stay

1 — 7 days

Healing

6 — 12 weeks

Frequently Asked Questions

You should consult an orthopedic specialist if you experience persistent joint pain lasting more than a few weeks, difficulty in walking or using your limbs, joint swelling or stiffness, numbness or tingling in arms or legs, sports injuries, or any deformity. Early consultation often leads to better outcomes and more treatment options.

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical technique where the surgeon operates through small keyhole incisions using a tiny camera (arthroscope) and specialized instruments. Benefits include less pain, minimal scarring, shorter hospital stay, and faster recovery compared to open surgery. Dr. Hakam Singh is fellowship-trained in arthroscopy from Ortho One, Coimbatore.

ACL recovery is a 9-12 month process. Crutches for 2-4 weeks, progressive rehabilitation through phases, jogging at 3 months, agility drills at 4-5 months, and return to sport at 8-9 months. Dr. Hakam Singh uses objective criteria (strength testing, hop tests) — not just time — to determine return-to-sport readiness.

Yes, the goal of ACL reconstruction is to restore knee stability for return to sport. Most athletes return to pivoting sports between 6-9 months after surgery, provided they complete rehabilitation and pass functional testing. Returning before 9 months doubles the re-injury risk.

Yes, joint replacement is one of the most successful surgical procedures in modern medicine. Success rates exceed 95%. Complications are rare and most are treatable. Modern techniques minimize tissue damage and blood loss for faster recovery.

Most back pain (80-90%) improves with conservative treatment within 6-12 weeks. Surgery is considered only for persistent nerve compression causing leg pain, weakness, or numbness, or for structural problems like severe spinal stenosis. Dr. Hakam Singh always recommends the most conservative appropriate treatment first.

Please bring any X-rays, MRI/CT reports and discs, previous medical records, a list of current medications, your insurance details, and a brief written summary of your symptoms including when they started, what makes them better or worse, and any previous treatments tried.

Key prevention strategies include: structured neuromuscular warm-up programs (FIFA 11+) which reduce ACL injuries by 50-70%, regular strength training 2-3 times per week, proper load management (gradual increase in training), adequate sleep and nutrition, appropriate footwear and protective equipment, and listening to your body for warning signs of injury.
